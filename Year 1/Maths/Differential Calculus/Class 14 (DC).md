Limits
---

$$\displaylines{

\lim_{x\to c} x = c \\

\lim_{x\to c} x^n = c^n \\

}$$


$$\displaylines{

f, g \text{ are functions} \\
\lim_{x\to c} f(c) = l \\
\lim_{x\to c} g(c) = k \\

\lim_{x\to c} f(g(c)) = \lim_{x\to c} g(c) + \lim_{x\to c} f(c) \\

\lim_{x\to c} g \times f = l\times k \\
\lim_{x\to c} \frac{f(c)}{g(c)} = \frac{l}{k} \\
}$$

Assumptions: ( $c$ ) is a constant and ( $\lim_{{x \to a}} f(x)$ ) and ( $\lim_{{x \to a}} g(x)$ ) exist

$$
\begin{align*}
1. & \lim_{{x \to a}} [f(x) + g(x)] = \lim_{{x \to a}} f(x) + \lim_{{x \to a}} g(x) & \text{The limit of a sum is equal to the sum of the limits.} \\
2. & \lim_{{x \to a}} [f(x) - g(x)] = \lim_{{x \to a}} f(x) - \lim_{{x \to a}} g(x) & \text{The limit of a difference is equal to the difference of the limits.} \\
3. & \lim_{{x \to a}} cf(x) = c \lim_{{x \to a}} f(x) & \text{The limit of a constant times a function is equal to the constant times the limit of the function.} \\
4. & \lim_{{x \to a}} [f(x) g(x)] = \lim_{{x \to a}} f(x) \times \lim_{{x \to a}} g(x) & \text{The limit of a product is equal to the product of the limits.} \\
5. & \lim_{{x \to a}} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \frac{\lim_{{x \to a}} f(x)}{\lim_{{x \to a}} g(x)} & \text{The limit of a quotient is equal to the quotient of the limits (if \( \lim_{{x \to a}} g(x) \neq 0 \)).} \\
6. & \lim_{{x \to a}} [f(x)]^n = [\lim_{{x \to a}} f(x)]^n & \text{where \( n \) is a positive integer.} \\
7. & \lim_{{x \to a}} c = c & \text{The limit of a constant function is equal to the constant.} \\
8. & \lim_{{x \to a}} x = a & \text{The limit of a linear function is equal to the number \( x \) is approaching.} \\
9. & \lim_{{x \to a}} x^n = a^n & \text{where \( n \) is a positive integer.} \\
10. & \lim_{{x \to a}} n^x = n^a & \text{where \( n \) is a positive integer \& if \( n \) is even, we assume that \( a > 0 \).} \\
11. & \lim_{{x \to a}} n f(x) = n \lim_{{x \to a}} f(x) & \text{where \( n \) is a positive integer \& if \( n \) is even, we assume that \( \lim_{{x \to a}} f(x) > 0 \).}
\end{align*}
$$
$\textbf{Direct Substitution Property:}$
If $f$ is a polynomial or rational function and \( $a$ \) is in the domain of $f$ , then ( $\lim_{{x \to a}} f(x) = f(a)$\).

$\textbf{Simpler Function Property:}$
If $f(x) = g(x)$) when \( $x \neq a$) then $\lim_{{x \to a}} f(x) = \lim_{{x \to a}} g(x)$, as long as the limit exists.
